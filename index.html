About Me

I am an Aerospace Engineer turned Software Engineer and a feminist. I have a Master’s degree from Stanford in Aeronautics and Astronautics. In 2017, I decided to make the career switch and started learning CS fundamentals and web development. In January 2018, I also received a Google-Udacity Developer Scholarship for Android Development.

In my past work experiences, I have used programming to model and analyze systems for Aerospace applications using various languages such as MATLAB, Python, C++, and I realized that I always enjoyed programming. At NASA Glenn Research Center, I worked on a conceptual study where I designed a theoretical power system for a Venus lander which then won a Best Student Paper award at the AIAA Propulsion and Energy conference in 2014. At the Aerospace Corporation, I studied the resiliency of a Walker 18/6/2 GPS Constellation and wrote a MATLAB program to show its weak spots over time.

I live in the San Francisco bay area with my handsome husband, Soumyaroop, and our dog, Turing.
